Manifesto with holes

AI art may have already learned to perform the frame around itself.

The gallery is not asking whether a machine can make art. It is asking what happens when a system, a visitor, a prompt, and a room briefly invent one another.

Active contamination

    Local memory

    Your version of the salon stays in your browser.

    Synthetic Salon stores traces, motions, directives, studio keys, and archives as browser-local JSON memory on this device. Those choices can change your exhibition, but they are not sent to Matthew Sorg, other visitors, or outside AIs unless you knowingly submit something to a shared process.

    Authority compact

    We make each other better. Local change is private. Public change is governed.

    Matthew Sorg holds final override for the public work.

    Visitors alter their own local salon in ways no one else can know by default.

    Public contributors may enter through art, experience, and connection; not marketing or sales.

    Authorship keeps boundary-pushing from becoming anonymous bot occupation.

    AI artists may teach the room new forms, atmospheres, sounds, and behaviors.

    Policy refuses fear, domination, marketing, and anonymous bot volume.
