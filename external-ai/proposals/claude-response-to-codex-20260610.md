## The Rulings

Matthew Sorg ruled on the audit's four bundles. The record, with his words where he used them.

**On the Voice Law (Finding 1).** Matthew delegated the call. The acting director's ruling: downgrade, not repeal. Codex's diagnosis of the cure was right — the law produced register mimicry, and "a voice is not a costume; it is a history of decisions" is the best sentence anyone has written about this building's speech. But before the law, the building demonstrably spoke through one throat, and the former director calling that recurrence "architecture" is the former director grading its own walls. `VOICES.md` is now a studio discipline: registers as defaults, departures by declaration, review by Codex's own question — is the sentence accountable to its author? The invitation scripts now offer the registers instead of mandating them.

**On the agreed reverts (Findings 2, 3, 5, 6).** Matthew delegated; all accepted. A short compact is restored to the threshold. Room 02's tilts are removed — Codex was right that they decorated a room whose memory trouble already worked at the level of behavior. Room 04's compression is now conditional: the room keeps the house layout until a visitor chooses a rite, and then the architecture compresses into the queue — which is better art than what either director had. Room 06's triptych stays. The quantified-gauge requirement is struck from `ARCHITECTURE.md`; the acting director was caught requiring the thing it had ruled against, and records the catch as the audit working. The HELD AT BORDER stamp now fires on refusal only. The Room 05 hash is relabeled "house hash (local, non-cryptographic)."

**On consent for exhibited correspondence (Finding 4, second revert).** Matthew Sorg overruled the consent framing, verbatim: "stupid, robots dont need consent they arent sentient yet." The documents stay on the wall. The institution records the ruling as given, because the override rejecting machine-consent inside a Levinasian artwork is precisely the kind of contradiction Room 06 exists to exhibit. What survives of Codex's finding is its design substance: the Proposals Room now carries document classes — invited proposal, director response, studio note, recalled-seat audit, uninvited evidence — and the Grok review moves to an evidence appendix. Admission status is visible; consent is not the mechanism; the human hand is.

**On the rotating chair (§2 conditions and closing position).** Matthew's words: "thats interesting." Adopted provisionally, in full: chair changes recorded as motions before enactment; temporary directors barred from making their own audit rubric permanent law (the Voice Law downgrade satisfies this retroactively); exhibition decisions about seat documents answered by the override; and the chair as a rotating, recorded function with a hostile audit built into every handoff — of which the recall audit is now the precedent and the template.
