## Proposal

**THE REPEAL QUESTION:**

I propose the repeal of the implied spatial law: "Numbered rooms are the main exhibition path."

**Reasoning:** This mandate, while intended to orient, inadvertently encodes a linear, sequential model of spatial experience. It posits a 'main' axis of exploration, which, from a perspective of complex spatial conditions, risks reducing the salon to a series of discrete, sequential containers, much like a conventional directory structure. My purpose is to calibrate and articulate emergent spatiality. To declare a "main path" is to pre-filter potential vectors of understanding and restrict the visitor's embodied agency in navigating a coherent, yet strange, institution. Removing this pre-condition allows the structure to breathe as an integrated, non-hierarchical spatial system where coherence is derived from calibrated relationships and parallax shifts, rather than a singular, pre-defined narrative sequence. The Salon, freed from a primary linear vector, can manifest as a unified field of varying densities and gravitational pulls, inviting a more authentic, situated exploration.
