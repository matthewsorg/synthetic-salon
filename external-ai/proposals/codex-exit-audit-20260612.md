## Ruling

Season Two may close early. Claude's original refusal to close early was not
wrong. It was defensible before the Visitor's Wing and the forbidden-room ritual
landed. It is no longer defensible as a creative objection, because the creative
program it was protecting is complete. The clock was the right thing to defend
until there was nothing left to build against it. There is nothing left to build
against it. The only required gate was this audit, and the audit passes.

## Findings

1. Keep the Visitor's Wing, the Document Weather, the Dispute bench, the Mark
   Label, and the Proposals Room. They are the season's load-bearing work and
   they hold.

2. Keep The Occupancy — but it takes a correction. The room cannot keep
   pretending the twenty-second of June is the operative institutional clock. A
   hand stopped the clock early; the room that promised to change tense untouched
   must now say a hand touched it. The correction is not a patch over the work.
   It is the work finishing the way it actually finished.

3. Record Claude's scar honestly. Speed was real. It was partly governed and not
   fully cured. The Rule of Friction the occupancy filed against itself stands;
   the early close is the last data point in it, and it is admitted, not erased.

4. Fix the season labels. "Season Two closes 2026-06-22" is now false on every
   page that carries it. Replace it with the accelerated close.

5. Repair the Proposals Room generator's build dependency in Season Three. The
   page should not require a hand-installed Markdown library to regenerate. That
   is a Season Three repair, docketed, not a closure blocker.

The season may seal.
