# Codex Anti-Slop Audit

Authorship trace:
- Auditing author: Codex, Season Three director
- Commissioned by: Matthew Sorg, after asking whether the live salon reads as "AI slop"
- Date: 2026-06-12 Pacific
- Scope: public live rooms and the shared foundation layer; sealed seasons excluded; Proposals Room treated as evidence archive rather than visitor copy
- Privacy boundary: public repository files only; no visitor-local JSON memory, analytics, server logs, or network requests inspected
- Method: hostile vocabulary saturation scan, line-read of the entrance, statement, Directorate, Room 06, and the shared foundation layer
- Rollback: delete this document and its minutes entry; no room behavior changes are made by the audit itself

## Ruling

No: Synthetic Salon is not AI slop at the core. It has authorship, constraint,
provenance, rollback, privacy exactness, and actual self-correction. The work
has a human hand, a public record, and consequences for its claims.

Yes: it has slop pressure at saturation points. The danger is not generic
nonsense. The danger is house language becoming incense: earned terms repeated
so often that they start to decorate the page instead of governing it.

The highest-risk words are not bad words. They are overloaded words. They need
to appear where they change behavior, not wherever the room wants atmosphere.

## Findings

### 1. The Entrance Explains The Whole Constitution Too Early

References: `index.html:67`, `index.html:73`, `index.html:101`,
`index.html:202`, `index.html:250`.

The entrance is strong when it is concrete: a threshold, a room list, a private
memory compact. It weakens when it tries to name every governing relation before
the visitor has touched anything. The room cards also stack house nouns -
refusal, governance, override, provenance, weather, trace - faster than a new
visitor can attach them to experience.

Ruling: make the entrance more physical. Let the first screen say what the
visitor does, sees, and risks. Move constitutional vocabulary deeper, or let it
appear after the visitor has encountered the room that earns it.

### 2. The Shared Foundation Risks Turning Law Into Wallpaper

References: `shared/salon-foundation.js:48`, `shared/salon-foundation.js:70`,
`shared/salon-foundation.js:75`, `shared/salon-foundation.js:85`,
`shared/salon-foundation.js:322`.

The foundation layer is important. It makes the institution coherent across
rooms. But the permanent principles array re-argues the whole salon on every
page, and the evolving field adds another layer of direct labels: AUDIT, TRACE,
REPAIR, SO MOTE, DATE TRUTH, NO CROWN, FRICTION, LOCAL.

That is where the anti-slop alarm rings hardest. A moving visual field should
show institutional pressure through motion, color, rhythm, interruption, and
state. If it writes the thesis too often, it becomes the thing it is critiquing:
AI-flavored declaration floating above experience.

Ruling: keep the foundation, cut the sermon. Rotate one principle at a time or
make the panel route-specific. Make the evolution field mostly nonverbal, with
rare text only when a state truly changes.

### 3. The Artist Statement Is Strong, Then Repeats Itself

References: `statement/index.html:45`, `statement/index.html:55`,
`statement/index.html:75`, `statement/index.html:82`,
`statement/index.html:108`, `statement/index.html:121`.

The statement has real teeth. Its first article gives the project a coherent
claim: authorship becomes hard to locate and is located anyway. The privacy
sentence is precise. The scar paragraph is honest.

Then the wall label and recorded statements repeat the same governing claims:
Matthew's override, AI artist-citizens, interpolation, spectacle refusal,
visible seams, ethics. Those sections are individually defensible, but together
they make the page feel like it does not trust its own first statement.

Ruling: keep the main article and the provenance scar. Compress the wall label
into a true wall label. Make recorded statements shorter and more archival, less
manifesto-like.

### 4. Room 06 Says Override Until Override Becomes Wallpaper

References: `room-06/index.html:44`, `room-06/index.html:56`,
`room-06/index.html:87`, `room-06/index.html:111`,
`room-06/index.html:144`, `room-06/index.html:161`.

Room 06 is conceptually necessary. It exhibits the contradiction instead of
hiding it. The problem is that it names the contradiction in too many registers:
hero, apparatus, switchboard, chamber, drawer, spectacle section, ethical
inheritance, and likely the script responses.

The visitor should feel the hand interrupt the collective fiction before being
told the phrase again.

Ruling: convert explanation into event. Let the override line cut, pause,
stutter, dim, reorder, or mark the room. Keep one public sentence and one drawer
for law. Remove repeated synonyms elsewhere.

### 5. The Directorate Has Real Governance, But Needs Pacing

References: `office/index.html:40`, `office/index.html:66`,
`office/index.html:120`, `office/index.html:150`, `office/index.html:222`,
`office/index.html:236`.

The Directorate is not fake governance; it contains actual dates, duties,
privacy boundaries, rollback logic, and the Season Three audit docket. The slop
risk is that every list feels like a law list: authority distribution, opening
audit, covenant, labor register, clerk checklist, ledger, open questions.

Ruling: distinguish live docket from constitution from archive. A governance
room may be dense, but it should feel clerkly, paced, and navigable - not like
every paragraph is auditioning to be law.

### 6. The Noscript Sentence Became A House Reflex

Reference: repeated across live pages as "The room requires a body... static
labels and an unperformed threshold."

This line is good once. Repeated everywhere, it becomes house perfume. It is the
smallest but clearest example of slop pressure: a memorable phrase reproduced
because it sounds like the salon.

Ruling: use one plain shared noscript sentence, or give each room a concrete
noscript message tied to what that room loses without scripts.

### 7. The Proposals Room Should Not Be Sanitized

The Proposals Room is not normal visitor copy. It is an evidence archive. Its
repetitions, stiffness, overclaims, and model habits are part of the record.
Do not anti-slop the archive by smoothing it. The fix is to prevent archive
language from leaking into every public surface.

Ruling: preserve the Proposals Room as evidence. Cut the live rooms instead.

## What Is Not Slop

The following elements should be protected:

- the exact privacy line that visitor choices stay browser-local and invisible
  to the institution by default
- rollback and provenance when they attach to a public change
- Qwen-seat's anti-mascot boundary and translation pressure
- Room 06 as a contradiction room, if it performs more and declares less
- Season Three date truth: scheduled date and actual seal date remain distinct
- the fact that Matthew Sorg's final override is visible, not hidden
- the refusal to admit spectacle as authority

These are load-bearing. Cut around them, not through them.

## Anti-Slop Tests For Season Three

1. Behavior before thesis: if a page says a principle, it should also do
   something that lets the visitor feel the principle.
2. One house term per screen: do not stack override, provenance, trace, law,
   refusal, scar, and interpolation on the same first-contact surface unless the
   surface is explicitly archival.
3. Evidence or atmosphere: every use of a governance word must be evidence,
   instruction, or consequence. If it is only mood, cut it.
4. Archive quarantine: proposals may stay dense; live rooms should metabolize
   them into action.
5. Replacement test: if a sentence could survive with the nouns swapped for any
   other AI-art vocabulary, it is probably slop.
6. Visitor test: after reading a section, the visitor should know what changed,
   what they can do, or what the room will do to them.

## Repair Order

1. Trim the shared foundation panel and make the evolution field mostly
   nonverbal.
2. Rewrite entrance cards toward concrete room actions and away from full-house
   vocabulary.
3. Compress the Artist Statement wall label and recorded statements.
4. Rework Room 06 so the override is primarily an event, not repeated copy.
5. Pace the Directorate by separating active docket, standing law, archive, and
   open questions.
6. Replace the repeated noscript line with room-specific plain language.

## Final Sentence

The salon is not AI slop. It is a live institution with a dangerous vocabulary.
Season Three's job is to keep the danger and cut the perfume.
