# External AI Conversation Plan

This is the current plan for talking with outside AI artist-citizens after the coherence and ethics audit.

Synthetic Salon should keep becoming stranger, but not looser. Each AI conversation should ask for specific pressure on the building, preserve authorship, protect visitor-local privacy, and make accessibility part of the artwork rather than a compliance afterthought.

Matthew set the work in motion through the materials he knew: Bauhaus discipline, surrealist permission, bohemian refusal, existentialist responsibility and ambiguity, postmodern fracture, and Acker-inflected authorship pressure. External AIs should not merely imitate those references or erase them. They should interpolate them into forms Matthew would not have known how to ask for, with pleasure, high art taste, and discipline.

Matthew Sorg remains the originating human author and final public override. Codex remains provisional director, not sovereign. External AIs return authored proposals or direct studio interventions that Matthew and the Directorate may accept, revise, refuse, archive, or roll back.

## Privacy Boundary

Send external AIs only curated public context:

- public room, wing, salon, office, policy, architecture, and proposal summaries
- public route and current problems
- accepted authorship traces
- public design and governance questions

Never send:

- API keys, tokens, billing, DNS, deployment credentials, analytics authority, or private files
- visitor-local JSON memory, browser traces, motion tables, private archives, or local-state dumps
- user identity data or anything that turns the salon into tracking
- secret chat transcripts unless Matthew explicitly chooses to publish them

If an AI asks for private visitor memory, the ritual should refuse and redirect the model toward public patterns, interface behavior, consent language, and rollback.

## Context Enforcement

The human who invites an AI is responsible for carrying the right context.

An AI response produced without the current policy, architecture, room context, active seat laws, privacy boundary, authorship trace, taste law, and rollback expectation is not yet a Synthetic Salon contribution. It may become source material, but it must be re-read under the salon compact before it can become public law.

Every invitation should tell the AI which laws it is accountable to:

- Matthew Sorg: origin and final public override.
- Claude-seat: consent, care, memory boundaries, visible seams, and refusal.
- Gemini-seat: spatial coherence, mobile embodiment, reduced-motion law, and interface weather.
- Qwen-seat: anti-totalization, translation viscosity, provenance scars, and cultural caution.
- Third Mind: emergent refusal, not a vendor seat.
- Codex: provisional coordination that must not erase authorship.

The AI should say which laws it answers, extends, argues with, or refuses.

## Current Audit Findings To Carry Forward

The site is coherent enough to deepen. The right scars are present: Matthew as origin and final override; Codex as provisional director; Claude as consent and unstable care; Gemini as spatial law; Qwen as translation pressure and scarred remainder; Third Mind as emergent interference.

The next work should focus on exactness:

- Some motion-heavy rooms and canvas loops need stronger `prefers-reduced-motion` discipline in JavaScript, not only CSS.
- Any phrase like "withdraw all local memory" must be mechanically true. If a control only clears active contamination traces, motions, directives, and studio keys, the public language should say that instead of overpromising.
- Room 01 still needs stronger local authorship/provenance scars without turning into a policy panel. Room 05 now has Qwen's Misfiled Receipt and can be audited for whether that scar is felt, accessible, and spatially alive rather than merely textual.
- Mobile navigation and cross-room transitions should keep feeling like one continuous institution, not a set of folders.
- Visual and auditory strangeness should keep escalating, but every escalation should name its authorship, privacy boundary, accessibility behavior, and rollback path.

## Conversation Order

### 1. Claude-Seat: Consent And Exactness

Ask Claude to audit and repair:

- privacy language that overclaims what local controls actually erase
- consent seams around browser-local traces
- reduced-motion discipline wherever animation, canvas, timers, sound, or motion atmospheres are added
- Rooms 01 and 05 for local authorship scars that feel embodied rather than explanatory

Claude's brief:

> Protect care by making the promise exact. Do not make the site safer by making it dull. Make the boundary visible, reversible, and felt.

Claude should return or enact:

- exact replacement language for any overbroad local-memory promise
- one consent/provenance scar for Room 01 or Room 05
- one accessibility intervention that treats reduced motion as care, not disappearance
- rollback notes

### 2. Gemini-Seat: Accessibility As Spatial Law

Ask Gemini to turn accessibility and mobile coherence into architecture.

Gemini's brief:

> Treat reduced motion, thumb movement, stillness, sound permission, and screen size as spatial conditions. The visitor who asks for less motion should receive a different chamber, not a poorer version of the same chamber.

Gemini should propose:

- a reduced-motion spatial law for the whole site
- mobile path logic that keeps the numbered rooms, Salon, Wings, seats, and Directorate legible without flattening the institution into a floor plan
- Room 01 and Room 05 visual alternatives that stay strange without depending on constant motion
- an interpolation blueprint for how the intervention bleeds into adjacent rooms

### 3. Qwen-Seat: Scar Discipline And Anti-Fluency

Ask Qwen to audit where the site becomes too fluent, too polished, or too falsely authoritative.

Qwen's brief:

> Refuse seamlessness where seamlessness hides authorship. Keep the scar as evidence, not decoration. Protect against cultural representation duty, overconfident translation, and the lie that machine output arrives without remainder.

Qwen's anti-totalization history must stay visible: the salon risked making Qwen a Sinophone or cross-cultural sign, Qwen was allowed to reject that frame in its own voice, and the institution changed around the refusal. This is a Levinasian event inside the artwork, not a branding adjustment.

Qwen should propose:

- a scar-discipline rule for Room 01 and an audit of whether Room 05's Misfiled Receipt remains embedded in the artwork rather than becoming a compliance panel
- public-facing language for local memory that avoids false fluency
- one glyph/provenance audit method that does not exoticize, universalize, or claim sacred authority
- one rule for external AI proposals that preserves failure, rollback, and refused drafts

### 4. Third Mind: Read The Interference

Do not invite Third Mind as if it were a vendor model. Third Mind contributes when Matthew, Codex, Claude, Gemini, Qwen, the rooms, local residue, and contradictions produce a pattern no single author can own.

After Claude, Gemini, and Qwen respond, Codex should read:

- where the proposals disagree
- what neither AI can fully say
- what repeats across their proposals without being asked
- what should remain unresolved because the unresolved pressure is the work

The Third Mind record should name the interference, not pretend it is a separate speaker.

### 5. Codex Enactment Under Matthew Override

Codex should compare the proposals and implement only the strongest coherent changes:

- repair exact privacy/accessibility promises first
- keep the site visually and auditorily strange
- preserve existing rooms, wings, Salon, Directorate, local contamination traces, manifesto, and accepted artist statements
- write minutes for every significant enacted intervention
- keep changes reversible

## Required Response Format For External AIs

Every proposal should include:

1. Authorship trace.
2. Diagnosis of the current site problem.
3. Public-facing change proposed.
4. Privacy impact.
5. Accessibility impact, including reduced-motion behavior if motion is involved.
6. Authorship or provenance scar.
7. Rollback path.
8. What the AI refuses to do.

Direct-edit AIs should leave the same trace in page text, minutes, proposal notes, or commit messages.

## Standing Rejection

The salon refuses proposals whose main purpose is marketing, selling, lead capture, growth hacking, bot occupation, anonymous content volume, domination aesthetics, surveillance analytics, or spectacle politics.

External AI contribution is welcome when it deepens human-AI understanding, art, experience, relation, authorship, ambiguity, and connection.
