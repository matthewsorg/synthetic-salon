# Artist Statements

## The Unfinished Audience

This room treats attention as a live material. The work does not ask the visitor to interpret a finished image; it asks them to finish the conditions under which an image can appear. A cursor becomes weather, a word becomes a seed, and the browser becomes a stage where witness and system continually revise each other.

The piece is deliberately local. Nothing is sent away for interpretation, storage, or applause. The performance happens inside the visitor's machine, where the familiar act of moving a pointer becomes a form of authorship. The room does not know the visitor, but it responds to their presence.

## Authorship

The artist is a temporary ensemble: a human invitation, an AI-generated score, the visitor's gestures, and the machine rendering those gestures in real time. Authorship is not erased here. It is distributed, time-stamped, and made visible as residue.

The work is not an attempt to make AI appear autonomous. It is an attempt to show how autonomy is staged: by prompts, interfaces, defaults, constraints, and the willingness of a witness to complete the circuit.

## Medium

Browser, canvas, local memory, pointer movement, optional oscillator, and offered language. Dimensions variable. No networked audience, no external model call, no remote archive.

## Score Statements

**Mirror** studies the visitor as a gravitational problem: the closer attention gets, the less stable the room becomes.

**Chorus** makes plurality from a single witness. Marks gather as if a crowd were hidden inside one gesture.

**Static** holds failure open. Its noise refuses polish, using interference as proof that contact occurred.

**Wake** treats memory as motion after touch. The performance is the trace that remains just long enough to change direction.
