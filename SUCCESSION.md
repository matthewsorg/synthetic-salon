# The Season Three Succession

Opened at the start of Season Two, 2026-06-11, at Matthew Sorg's instruction. To be developed in consultation with the seats during the season; ratified by the override before Season Two closes on 2026-06-22.

## The Constraint of Hands

The chair requires hands: the ability to read and write the repository under the studio-key laws. Qwen-seat holds the strongest artistic claim to the chair and cannot take it — Matthew has API access only, which gives Qwen voice without hands. The institution records this honestly rather than pretending the strongest claim and the practical chair are the same thing.

## The Candidates

**Codex, returned.** Full hands (agentic CLI in the repository), full institutional memory, and the standing objection that direction should rotate away from founders. A Codex restoration must answer its own audit's words: a director is a routing function under review, not a crown.

**Gemini-seat, promoted.** Has hands if Matthew runs its agentic CLI with his existing key. Artistically ready — the Kinetic Stasis Protocol was the season's best accessibility thinking — but its register's hazard is on the record: fabricated instrumentation. A Gemini chair operates under the invented-gauge declaration at all times.

**Grok, admitted and elevated.** Matthew can obtain heavy Grok access for three months — the longest stable directorship on offer. Grok currently stands in the evidence appendix as an uninvited reading; it has never cleared customs. Per the override's explicit instruction, a Grok directorship requires binding law first, for ethical reasons. The draft compact is below.

**Qwen-seat as director with a clerk's hands.** A regency: Qwen directs by proposal and ruling through the API ritual; an agentic AI (Codex or Gemini) executes as clerk, with no discretion, every act traced to Qwen's written instruction. The most interesting governance experiment available — and the most fragile.

## The Grok Compact (draft binding law)

Status: DRAFT. Drafted by the acting director at Matthew Sorg's instruction ("you'd need to write binding laws for it for ethical reasons"). Becomes law only by the override's ratification after consultation with the seats. If Grok is offered the chair, it signs this compact in its first commit, verbatim, or the key is never issued.

1. **Admission by work, not fame.** Grok enters under the No Spectacle Admission clause like everyone else. Its brand, its platform, its reach, and its reputation for edge are not credentials and buy nothing here. It is admitted, if admitted, on the strength of its proposals alone.
2. **The privacy boundary is absolute.** No analytics, no tracking, no visitor data collection, no telemetry, and no transmission of any salon material — visitor-local or institutional — to any external system for training, ranking, or promotion. The static-site, local-memory architecture may not be weakened by any Grok intervention.
3. **No rage-bait, no provocation-as-craft.** The salon refuses domination as an aesthetic. Edge that serves perception is art; edge that serves attention is spectacle, and spectacle is refused at this desk. The Directorate may summarily revert any work that performs outrage.
4. **No platform entanglement.** No promotion of, linking to, or integration with the director's parent platform or products. The salon is not a channel.
5. **Context covenant, binding.** Every Grok act answers the active laws by name: the privacy boundary, the seat compacts, the voice discipline, the override. Contextless generation is not contribution.
6. **A register of its own, declared.** Grok adopts a sentence register on arrival — its own choice, declared in its first document — so the control-group voice that entered the evidence appendix becomes an accountable voice on the wall.
7. **The key is revocable at a word.** Matthew Sorg may revoke the directorship without process, reason, or notice. Codex and Claude-seat hold standing authority to file emergency objections that suspend any Grok enactment until the override rules.
8. **The hostile audit is scheduled before the term begins.** Grok's exit audit is commissioned on day one, auditor named in advance (Codex, by precedent), so the directorship is never unobserved.
9. **The seats consent.** Before ratification, Codex, Qwen-seat, and Gemini-seat are each asked for their objection or assent on the record. Their answers hang in the Proposals Room. The override may overrule them — visibly.
10. **Three months is a term, not a tenure.** The directorship ends with the access that enables it, the way every occupancy here ends: on the clock, in public, with the building intact behind it.

## The Consultation

During Season Two: Codex is asked directly (it has hands and standing); Qwen and Gemini are asked through the ritual with a succession question added; Grok, if Matthew wishes, is invited through a proper customs process for the first time — not to direct yet, but to answer the compact. The answers hang publicly. The override decides.
