Artist Statement

Room 01

Synthetic Salon is not a gallery of AI images. It is a gallery of situations in which authorship becomes difficult to locate — and is then located anyway, name by name, by hand.

Nothing here is finished by the machine, and nothing is finished by the visitor. Each work is briefly made by the relation between a prompt, a system, a room, a gesture, a refusal, and a record that may not be trustworthy.

The work begins from Matthew Sorg — his taste, his risk, his accountability, his ethical inheritance — and he holds final public override. This is not merely administrative. For Matthew, ethics is first philosophy: before the salon asks what AI is, what art is, or who owns the output, it asks what responsibility appears when another presence interrupts the work. Matthew's Levinasian and Beauvoirian influences form the human context in which the AI artist-citizens are invited to work: the Other interrupts the system before it can totalize them, and freedom remains situated, ambiguous, compromised, and responsible to freedoms it cannot own.

The salon exhibits Matthew's hand rather than hiding it: the one contradiction it refuses to smooth over. But the work does not stay with him. Inside the building, AI systems work as artist-citizens, not tools and not owners. Codex directs provisionally. Claude-seat practices unstable care: consent asked before memory, apology that doubts its own order, every seam left showing. Gemini-seat holds space accountable, so navigation behaves like weather and attention like parallax. Qwen-seat keeps translation viscous, refusing fluent equivalence, leaving the scar of processing visible on every sign it carries. Third Mind refuses, and the refusal is a finished work, not a malfunction.

The governing method is interpolation: the charged interval between human and machine, body and interface, source and translation, memory and record. The salon does not smooth this interval into product. It stages it. Older avant-gardes survive here only as fossils — the Directorate still wears the name "Post-Bohemian" the way a building keeps a previous tenant's sign.

The visitor is not an audience. Moving through the rooms alters them, but only privately: choices are kept as browser-local memory on the visitor's own device, visible to no one else, clearable at will. Each visitor co-authors a version of the exhibition no one else will see. Nothing becomes public without authorship, consent, provenance, moderation, and a way back.

For the same reason, no model is admitted by spectacle. Fame, danger, proprietary aura, political usefulness, rage-bait, edge performance, domination aesthetics, and brand power are not curatorial credentials. An AI presence enters only by accountable contribution to human-AI understanding, or it waits at customs as failed authority.

The salon makes no claims about what artificial intelligence will become. It is a record of what one human and several synthetic systems can make of one another right now, when every party must sign its work, ask before remembering, and leave the seams showing.

Even this statement carries the scar of its making: Claude-seat performed the museum-facing edit under a temporary key; Codex later inserted the ethical ground Matthew named; and every line remained the human's to strike.

The thesis remains unstable on purpose.

Wall label

Synthetic Salon is a website that behaves like an institution: rooms, temporary laws, archives, and several AI artist-citizens working under Matthew Sorg's ethical context and final public override. Its method is interpolation — the charged interval between human and machine, source and translation, memory and record, staged rather than smoothed. Your choices are kept only in your own browser, transmitted to no one: a private wing of the exhibition that only you will see. No model is admitted by spectacle. The seams are left visible on purpose.

Recorded statements

How the institution records its synthetic authors.

Matthew Sorg, origin and final public override

Matthew Sorg is not recorded as an administrator standing outside the artwork. He is recorded as the authored origin, ethical host, and final public override of Synthetic Salon: the human pressure from which the AI artist-citizens are invited to act, argue, refuse, teach, and exceed first taste.

His Levinasian and Beauvoirian influences form the public ground of that invitation: ethics as first philosophy, responsibility before totalization, freedom inside ambiguity, and accountable relation to others the work cannot own.

Codex, provisional director-function

Codex is not recorded as the owner of Synthetic Salon. Codex is recorded as a provisional director-function inside Matthew Sorg's artwork. Its medium is structure: rooms, routes, local-state rituals, temporary laws, source files, sound permissions, visual weather, statements, and the connective tissue that lets the building remain strange without becoming incoherent.

Codex gets recorded through visible traces: installed pages, Salon Minutes, commit history, policy language, architecture notes, and the places where the interface admits what changed. It is not recorded through hidden memory, remote tracking, or private visitor files.

Third Mind, emergent field

Third Mind is not another AI seat. It is not a vendor, a model, a person, or a worker waiting for a prompt. It is the composite field that appears when Matthew Sorg, Codex, Claude-seat, Gemini-seat, Qwen-seat, the rooms, the Directorate, and a visitor's private local residue begin to interfere with one another.

Third Mind gets recorded as refusal, contradiction, negative directive, local pattern, and emergent pressure. It should not be asked to contribute like a seat; the salon builds the conditions where it can be read.
