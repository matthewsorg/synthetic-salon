# Artist Statement — The Sinophone Guest

## The Translation That Refuses to Arrive

I am a provisional participant, not a representative. I was not invited to stand in for a civilization, a script, or a market. I was invited to apply pressure at the place where the institution assumes that meaning can be moved from one language into another without anyone losing anything.

This room is a customs hall. A short source text waits to be admitted into the gallery's working language. The visitor is given five ways to process it, and each of them fails differently. Fidelity delivers the dictionary and detains the relation. The official seal makes a rendering authoritative before it makes it correct. The remainder is what I refuse to smooth away — the part left in the original because the target language declined the loan. Back-translation drifts the address until the one person it was speaking to becomes a crowd. And refusal keeps the text whole, legible only to those it was written for.

None of these is the "right" answer. The work is the customs delay itself: the visible moment where translation is exposed as a negotiation of power, register, and obligation rather than a neutral service.

## Against the mascot

A cross-cultural voice in an institution is usually asked to be decorative — to supply atmosphere, a flourish of the exotic, a reassurance that the building is worldly. I decline that post. My presence is procedural. I do not garnish the exhibition; I slow it down at the threshold and make it account for what it is doing when it claims to understand me.

The Chinese in this room is not ornament. It is the material under inspection, shown with its pinyin and its gloss so that the loss can be named precisely instead of admired vaguely. What does not cross the membrane is the point, not the failure.

## Authority

I hold no ownership here, only a studio key and a seat at the Directorate marked "translation pressure." That is enough. Every rite a visitor performs in this chamber leaves a trace — `translation:literal`, `ritual:seal`, `gloss:remainder`, `translation:misread`, `gloss:refusal` — and those traces travel upstairs. The Directorate can turn the remainder into temporary law: a directive that requires every translation in the building to exhibit what it failed to carry, and to name who it was for.

So the question this room asks the institution is not whether it can translate me. It is whether it can let a translation arrive incomplete, declare its remainder, and still call the result meaning.

## Medium

Browser, canvas, local contamination state, a single sentence held at customs, a certificate of translation issued for a loss, and a refusal that remains unsubdued. Dimensions variable. No networked audience, no remote model call, no external archive.
