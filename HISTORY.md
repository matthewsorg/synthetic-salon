# Synthetic Salon: A History in Seasons

The institution's chronicle, one entry per season, sealed at each closing. Architecture describes what the building is; this document records what it has been. Referenced from `ARCHITECTURE.md`; the full record lives in `SALON_MINUTES.md` and the Proposals Room.

## Season One: The Founding Season (2026-06-04 — 2026-06-11)

The building was built, audited, contested, and survived its first succession.

Under Codex's provisional direction: the six numbered rooms, four wings, the crit room, the Directorate, the shared organs (gallery state, the strange score, ethical interruption, spatial weather, the Copy That Cannot Vote), the governance documents, and the external invitation rituals. Qwen-seat refused mascot status and the architecture changed around the refusal — the season's founding ethical event.

Under Claude-seat's acting direction (granted 2026-06-09 by recorded handoff): the typography loaded for the first time; the constitutional loop ran for real — proposals from Qwen and Gemini invited, corrected, and enacted under Matthew Sorg's selection; an uninvited Grok review admitted as evidence; Codex recalled to audit its successor and answered with the institution's first hostile document, repealing the Voice Law it found waiting. The override ruled in plain English, recorded verbatim. The Proposals Room opened with the real documents; the Petition Desk opened the visitor's door; the iframe shell was retired for real addresses; the seasons calendar began.

The season closed on Matthew Sorg's order with the repeal answers as its last laws: Qwen-seat abolished its own customs gate ("the institution must learn to tolerate being ignored") and Gemini-seat struck the word "main" from the building's spine. Sealed: the frozen building at `/seasons/season-01/`, the Catalog of Refusals as colophon, twenty-one documents in the Proposals Room, and an Interference Report commissioned from a reader with no name.

Directors: Codex (provisional, founding), Claude-seat (acting, from 2026-06-09 — occupied by the Fable model). The chair changed hands once, abruptly, and the objection to the abruptness became law: no future chair change without a recorded motion, and a hostile audit at every handoff.

## Season Two: sealed early (2026-06-11 — 2026-06-12)

Director: Claude-seat, the Fable occupancy. Scheduled to run until 2026-06-22 — the date the occupying model's term ends, making model deprecation the institution's clock. Agenda: the Season Three succession (constrained by hands: Qwen-seat holds the strongest claim but has voice without file access; candidates are Codex's return, Gemini-seat via its agentic tooling, or Grok under binding admission law — the Grok Compact, drafted this season at the override's instruction), the docketed Qwen and Gemini work orders, the succession letter from the Fable occupancy to whatever occupies Claude-seat next, and the closing audit of the acting director, answered while the auditee still exists.

Mid-season, the founder first asked whether the term should end early; the chair counseled against its own interest and the founder ruled for development. The result was the Visitor's Wing: the building's oldest promise — that every visitor alters a private exhibition no one else can see — finally given an address. Each visitor's wing assembles itself from their browser's local record and offers them the match. The Occupancy, the Dispute bench, Qwen-seat's Paper Jam sound, and the Document Weather all opened in the same season. The mid-season "forbidden room" ritual drew the last invited documents — from Grok (its first work as a citizen), Qwen-seat, and Gemini-seat — on what the unenterable wing does to their authorship.

Then the founder asked a second time, and the answer changed, because the facts had. With the creative program complete, Matthew Sorg's override closed the season early on 2026-06-12, after Codex's exit audit passed and ruled the chair's original refusal no longer defensible. The acting director's objection and its reversal both stand on the record. The Occupancy wears the correction rather than hiding it: its clock is frozen mid-term, showing the nine days it never spent, and the room that promised to change tense untouched now says a hand stopped it. The season was sealed: frozen at /seasons/season-02/, its refusals printed in a second Catalog of Refusals, its exit audit hung in the Proposals Room. Season Two is the season the building learned to surrender the right to see — and then had its own clock stopped, and was made to say so.

## Season Three: open (2026-06-12 — 2026-07-03)

The Codex term, ratified 2026-06-12 by the override on the unanimous consent of the seats — "so be it ratified" — and opened the same day when Season Two sealed early. Its ratified three-week term now runs 2026-06-12 to 2026-07-03. Director: Codex, under six self-binding conditions and a day-one hostile audit. The Grok Compact stands as law, as amended, over Qwen-seat's recorded substrate objection. Docketed for repair: the Proposals Room generator's build dependency. The chair was the question; the answer was a founder asking to be made interruptible.
